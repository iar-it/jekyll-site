Youth Admin Job Description

Jun 7, 2010

The Islamic Association of Raleigh (IAR) serves a growing Muslim community with an active base of over 3000 weekly participants. This is an active center providing services for both genders and all ages. Among the activities IAR coordinates are programs focusing on Muslim youth. Muslim youth programs and activities were originally designed to provide supplemental spiritual, social, educational, and recreational Islamic development for Muslim youth in order to tackle the ailments of being raised in an environment void of so many teachings of Allah.

There are existing programs for the youth such as Friday Night Program, Youth Camp, Youth Retreats, Youth Day, etc. These youth activities are currently being managed on a voluntary basis. In order to maximize the performance and efficiency of these programs, the IAR has created a "Brothers Youth Administrator" part time paid position to oversee and manage existing programs as well as add new programs.

Details

  • Job Title: Brothers Youth Administrator
  • Salary & Hours: $15 per hour
  • Reports to: Youth Committee Chairperson
  • Effective: 12/15/2010
  • Length of Agreement: 6 or 12 Month Contract
  • Programs Reference "MYC 2010 Youth Programs"

Job Summary

Brother's Youth Administrator will work directly with the Youth Committee Planning team on the planning of all existing and new programs.

Youth Administrator will be responsible for the execution of all the existing programs with the direct help of the part time Youth Coordinators for each age group.

Existing weekly programs and special activities

The existing weekly programs and special activities includes the Friday Night Youth Program, Weekly Halaqas, Saturday Program, Ramadan Programs, Camps/Retreats and Youth Day.

  • Secure and maintain the needed space for all activities and discussions for each activity whether it is held in the masjid or offsite
  • Ensure that all weekly youth programs are running smoothly and effectively for all age groups
  • Work with the Youth Coordinators on developing volunteer schedules and assignments to help each of them carry out their specific duties and responsibilities
  • Work with the Youth Coordinators to ensure a balance of fun activities and interesting/relevant discussions
  • Work with the Youth Coordinators on creating a general outline of topics/activities at least one month in advance for each program
  • Work with the Security team at the IAR to make sure that the premises remain safe for youth activities specifically for the Friday Night Youth Program
  • Must be present at all major programs.
  • Must attend each regular program at least once a month to ensure that all programs are carried out according to plan
  • Work with Treasurer of the Youth Committee to ensure that all programs are within assigned budgets and help account for all incoming deposits/fees collected by the Youth Coordinators for different programs
  • Work with the HS & College Youth Coordinators to ensure that the HS & College MSA clubs are receiving adequate assistance in planning and executing their own programs
  • Interview and present candidates for Youth Coordinator positions as needed
